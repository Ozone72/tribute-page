Neal Stephenson

Novelist | Futurist

Neal Stephenson talking about his book, 'Fall, or Dodge in Hell'

A Brief History of Neal:

Life Events

  • 1959 - Born on October 31 in Fort Meade, Maryland
  • 1981 - Graduates from Boston University (Go BU!). First specializing in physics, then switching to geography after he found that it would allow him to spend more time on the university mainframe. He graduated in 1981 with a B.A. in geography and a minor in physics.
  • 1984 - Publishes his first novel, 'The Big U', and moves to Seattle and the Pacific Northwest.

Novels

  • 1984 - The Big U
  • 1988 - Zodiac
  • 1992 - Snow Crash, a cyberpunk or post-cyberpunk novel fusing memetics, computer viruses, and other high-tech themes with Sumerian mythology, along with a sociological extrapolation of extreme laissez-faire capitalism and collectivism. Stephenson at this time would later be described by Mike Godwin as "a slight, unassuming grad-student type whose soft-spoken demeanor gave no obvious indication that he had written the manic apotheosis of cyberpunk science fiction."
  • 1994 - Interface (using psuedonym "Stephen Bury")
  • 1995 - The Diamond Age: Or, A Young Ladies Illustrated Primer - this book describes, in a way, the augmented reality currently being developed at Magic Leap and Microsoft.
  • 1996 - The Cobweb: A Novel
  • 1999 - Cryptonomicon - a novel including concepts ranging from Alan Turing's research into codebreaking and cryptography during the Second World War, to a modern attempt to set up a data haven. In 2013, Cryptonomicon won the Prometheus Hall of Fame Award. This is one of the best novels I have ever read. I became a lifelong fan.
  • 2003 - Quicksilver, volume I: The Baroque Cycle - Clarke Award winner, 2004; Locus SF Award nominee, 2004
  • 2004 - The Confusion, volume II: The Baroque Cycle – Locus SF Award winner, 2005
  • 2004 - The System of the World, volume III: The Baroque Cycle – Locus SF Award winner, 2005; Prometheus Award winner, 2005; Clarke Award nominee, 2005.
  • 2008 - Anathem – Locus SF Award winner, 2009; British Science Fiction Association Award nominee, 2008; Hugo and Clarke Awards nominee, 2009.
  • 2010-2012 - The Mongoliad: The Foreworld Saga
  • 2011 - REAMDE
  • 2015 - Seveneves - Hugo Award for Best Novel nominee.
  • 2017 - The Rise and Fall of D.O.D.O. - with Nicole Galland.
  • 2019 - Fall; or, Dodge in Hell: A Novel

Outside Work

  • 2000-2007 - Joins Jeff Bezos to work at Blue Origin where Neal worked on approaches to space, propulsion systems and business models.
  • 2014 - Stephenson was hired as Chief Futurist by the Florida-based company Magic Leap. Magic Leap developed a revolutionary form of portable spatial computing & augmented reality, not too different from technologies Stephenson previously has described in his science fiction books
